Chapter 7 — The Bistro

The bistro was just across the street. They stood stopped at the curb while waiting for the light. A car with a woman and her children in car seats in the back turned left with the green.

The bistro was dimly lit, warm and nearly empty.

“Bonjour. Table for two?" She said in English, but it sounded more French than English when she spoke it.

Marie, the hostess, turned immediately and led them past several tables then stopped at one with an outlet.

“…Is good?” Marie was already placing the menus and lighting the candle.

“Sure, this is fine.”

They ordered coffee and held the menus as Marie stepped away and took her place at the far end of the bar.

After a pregnant pause, she asked, “What are we doing again?”

"Like I said, first things first. I’ll be right back.”

He walked toward the far end of the bar.

Marie was still noting the coffee on the pad as he approached and looked up. “Was there something?”

Beth struggled with all the events of the evening, not enough pieces to a puzzle with the image on the back.

The waitress Vivienne brought the coffee, smiled, and walked back to the kitchen. The coffee was strong and needed cream.

She reached for her phone.

No messages.

She’d forgotten about the package. There it was in her tote.

“Cream and sugar?” Vivienne the waitress caught her off guard. She looked up and put her phone away.

“Would you like I wait?”

“Wait? Oh yes, give us a sec.”

At the end of the bar, Marie was writing something down and looked back at the table and smiled at her then walked toward the kitchen.

This was no way to spend your birthday she thought. Her shoes would never be the same and her feet hurt.

“There, all set.” He returned and sat across from her.

“Was that car following us?” She asked.

“I’m not sure, I think it’s gone.”

"What is that, that, whatever we got in the office.”

“All I know is, find this address look for the package, take it and the note. That’s all.”

“I thought you said it was just math, there’s more to it than that obviously.”

“Oh, I had to decipher that and find the keys, not as easy it looks. But seriously, like a treasure hunt as far as I know.”

“There should be something on the paper that we picked up to help with the next one.”

She looked a little skeptical.

“And I don’t know what that package is, I saw that guy drop it though as he passed and right there and I guess I got caught up in the moment.” He said.

She was reading the note from the item in the office.

"What is it?” He said.

“It has today’s date on it, and an address is written on the back and some other stuff 1956 and a Chinese character of something.”

“The note from the office?”

“Yes. I had to work it out. It was written in English. Who knew?”

“As I said, first things first. Cake and a little quiet reflection.”

“Bring on the cake. Or is there more quiet reflection?”

“Seriously, it’s just an accident or something, and that car was just a lady and her kids.”

“It’s ok, I guess I wasn’t expecting this much of an adventure. I wonder what it really was though, you’re right it’s probably nothing.”

“You have your heart pills.”

She laughed. “You’re funny. Ok, it has been fun. And I love a mystery.”

Just then Marie and the Vivienne brought two slices of semi-sweet chocolate cake with a few candles and two flutes of champagne.

“They didn’t have cherry pie and ice cream.”

“We’ll make do.”

“Bon Anniversaire!”

The cake was half sweet, the Champagne was too.

“The last time we had cake it was the chocolate cake and cherry coke bar our study group sponsored last New Years.”

“It was energy drinks mixed with Kaluha and Baileys over ice .. and s’mores.”

“No way.”

“Way.”

“Is that any good?”

She laughed. “Not really.”

“They ran out of cake by 10. Plan B. By then it didn’t really matter.”

“I don’t remember that at all.”

They both laughed.

“I always thought you’d end up running for something.” She said.

“Me, politics?”

“I’d vote for you. And I can think of at least one other person I could persuade to vote for you too,” she said.

“You were the political science major, not me.” He said.

“I never got the bug I guess. Someone once said, it’s not a sign on a door that makes a leader.”

“Who was that?” He asked.

“You.”

“Silly me. One of us needs to change the world, obviously the rest of the world is busy. I was hoping it was you.”

“You underestimate people.”

“Not usually. We’re used to getting to the bottom of things. Most of the world hasn’t got that kind of time.”

“Then it’s up to us.”

“Ok, as long as there’s cake at the end. I’ll do anything for cake, obviously.”

“I think I can arrange that.”

They talked and shared cake and after a while she took a deep breath. With a sigh she slipped off her shoes and just looked at him for a full minute while chasing some icing around on her plate with her fork.

“To you on your birthday.” He said.

“To many more.”

She decided it was a good birthday.
