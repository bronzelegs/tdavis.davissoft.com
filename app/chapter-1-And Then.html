Chapter 1: “And then I came home…”

Here in the hilly woods of New Jersey, it was quiet, peaceful. The city and its problems were just over the hills and across the river, but a world away.

It was dinner. It was a mid-week, busy work night, take out dinner.

They’d been married for a year. She was busy with her life, he was busy with his life, just like before the marriage but with shared laundry.

"Tell me a story.. and pass the mousse.”

It was her second helping of salmon; she wasn’t really hungry, but it went with the zin.

Dinner was an eclectic mix of takeout from a restaurant he’d passed on his way home from work.

It was a new place; an odd place with a Celtic name and Mediterranean themed dishes. It was his turn to shop for dinner.

“What kind of a story…” He said.

Her words fought to get out in between the mouthfuls of curried something or other.

“A good one of course. And the …” And she pointed. Around the table there were many dishes she didn’t recognize immediately. The scents and smells hinted at the complexities of the many delicacies.

“This?"

“No, the green stuff, what is that anyway…?"

“I have no clue, “ he said as he scooped some on top of the broccoli. He looked at her and sampled a bite of it, “Its good though. Are you sure you want to spoil it?”

She smiled and reached for the noodles. ”Give. And that too.” She and pointed at the salad.

“Once upon a time…"

She interrupted “Oh no, I get enough fairy stories at work, a story.”

“Does it need to have an ending?”

“Endings are good.. if it’s a… " she was looking for the veggies.

He finished her sentence “.. a good ending. Are there more noodles?”

"… over there, no the… ya, with the fork.”

He began. “I was at the grocer yesterday, or maybe it was Friday, anyway, the day I brought the roses.”

"Monday, it was Monday.” She was tapping her glass lightly just below the rim with the index finger of her left hand and not blinking.

“Ya Monday. Could have sworn it was Friday.”

She stopped tapping her wineglass.

“…what…?" He stopped eating.

“Nevermind… The story?" She swirled the wine glass and smiled.

“Who said this was the story? Maybe I’m just warming up. More mousse?” He held up his glass and did the same, then took a sip. She did the same.

He was reaching for something, she reached for the noodles. “No, leave it." He was pointing at something she wasn’t sure was actually food. He looked at her. She twisted up her nose and shook her head.

"Anyway, there was this car parked funny, sort of over the corner?”

There was a pregnant pause as he dished out noodles. The noodles were good, the green stuff she wasn’t sure.

“.. In The city, you know, near the park?”

“Thank you for the flowers." She interrupted and took a sip.

“…anyway … and the engine is running, and the door is open…”

“Front or back…"

“Backdoor, passenger side, are you sure you want to hear this?"

“Go on. You mean like right on the corner, or like off to the side."

“Just off to the side a little bit, like your sister parked it or something."

“Is my sister in this?”

“Well, no…" he smiled.

“.. and then what.”

She emptied her wine glass and refilled it with another half and started to refill his. It was a Zinfandel, it needed to breathe a little more and the spiciness curled on her tongue but she gulped a mouthful and set down her glass again.

“.. and then I came home."

“What?" She was grinning.

“Yeah, and then we had ..”.

“Yes, Monday, I remember,” she said. “Was that the story ?"

“Well yeah, it got me thinking..”

“No wait, you can’t call that a story.”

“I was thinking that it was unusual for that time of night, don’t you think?”

“What time was it?”

"I guess 6, maybe 6:30?“

“What kind of a car was it?" she asked and swirled her glass before taking another sip.

“Black… that kind the livery companies use, they look like unmarked cop cars sort of, but with darker windows.“

“Oh, those. Was there a driver?"

He shook his head. “Not that I saw, anyway.."

"You want any more of this?” She screwed up her nose and held up the green stuff then scooped up the last of noodles from the container.

"uh uh..”

“That it then?” she looked bored. “.. just a black car?”

“There was one other thing. I almost think I must have imagined it, that ever happen to you?"

“Like what?”

“Like, I dunno, it’s probably nothing.”

The phone rang.

“You get the phone, I’ll clean this up,” she said.

"Ok,… “ He got up and walked to the kitchen. Like most people, they still had an actual wired phone line, but it never rang anymore.

“.. save the mousse, it’s just cold." He said.

He picked up the handset and pressed a button.

“Hello…?”

She began by clearing the sunroom table and carried a stack of dishes to the kitchen and placed them on the marble countertop.

“Sure just a sec, hon it’s for you"…

She whispered, “Who is it."

Whispered ”…someone named Johnson?" And he set the handset down.

There was a knock at the door.

"ya, Hello?… speaking…”

"I’ll get the door .. ” and he kissed her cheek as he passed on the way to the foyer.

She whispered “Ok"

"Yes, that’s my, er, our address, who is this? What?”

She had green stuff on her thumb. She took the handset with her left hand and placed it next to her left ear. Someone was speaking.

“Pardon? Yes, that’s me, who is this?"

At the front door, the lock turned and the street noise filtered through to the kitchen.

She heard “How are you doing this evening sir, were you aware that…." And he opened one of the heavy wooden doors and stepped onto the brick landing outside.

“Yes, my husband is here too, what is this about?”

She noticed her husband was no longer visible in the foyer, then the person on the phone said something on the phone she didn’t catch.

“What’s that? No.” She said I don’t own a truck.”

She heard the front door swinging on the hinges with a creak, then the gate banging on the fence.

"I’m sorry you’ve got the wrong number. Goodbye.”

She hung up, distracted by the noise outside and continued clearing the sunroom table. She had a spatula and a takeout container in her hand. Next time she’d pick the restaurant she thought.

The rest of the takeout was going through the triage of eat, keep, toss when she realized she still heard street noise filtering in from the front door.

“Honey do you want any of this green stuff, there’s more in the bag.” She said loudly.

She scooped it into a container and looked at it at length.

“I have no idea what this stuff is, who would make this?” she said quietly to herself.

“Honey..?” she said, louder and in the direction of the foyer. She put down the container and started toward the front door.

In the living room she paused to look through the open front door. The front gate was open and the street visible beyond the fence.

A gust of wind had blown leaves and dust all over the entryway, the partially open front door creaked slightly in the wind.

“Honey, who is at the door.”

There was no answer.
