Chapter 4 — Taxi Driver

Across the river in Jersey City, a figure standing across the street from a coffee stand waited and as the light changed from green to red hurried across the emptying street diagonally toward a line of taxis parked outside.

And then, looking neither left or right with smooth motion, opened the door and got into the first cab, which had been left running.

Seconds later the taxi drove off and continued down the street and stopped at the next light two blocks away.

Just 6 blocks away Jamal’s Chicken Shack was featuring happy hour and World Cup play. It had recently reopened under new ownership. Formerly a one time a Taco Stand, and a convenience store now fried Chicken, wings, tap beer, and European football paid the rent.

Outside the front door, a utility pole and a phone-booth occupied the space between the diner and the street. There had been 3 booths in a row on this corner once. Two fell victim to a bus accident and were hauled away years ago. The third remained like the last lighter at a Ronald Reagan wake, disconnected, waiting for the morning that will never come.

Although ATT had stopped service to the phone-booth in the ’90s, it remained as a reminder of a slower time. The light still turned on when you closed the folding door.

“20 miles an hour ought to do it.”

The taxi was parked and idling a half a block back from the intersection, lights on, waiting for the stoplight to turn green with no traffic within a block.

As the light changed, the transmission slipped into gear and the taxi accelerated toward the utility pole.

“A good aim and a little luck..”

At 15 MPH and just across the midpoint of the intersection the car shifted into neutral, the door opened, and the driver rolled across the intersection.

The cab impacted the telephone pole, bounced off the pole barely pushing the phone-booth off its base on the front corner and lifting it slightly, breaking the mount holding it to the concrete slab.

The cab came to a rest at the curb just beyond the corner, the drivers’ door wide open.
