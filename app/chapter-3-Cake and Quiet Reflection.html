Chapter 3 — Cake and Quiet Reflection

They were to meet at the fountain on 66th At 5 PM, the decision about where to after that was up to them. But that is not what happened totally.

She was there in front of the fountain looking chilled and apprehensive, the lights that were illuminating the sprays had just clicked on.

He saw her standing there. He smiled and walked up.

“Beth, how great to see you, gee it’s been too long. Sorry, Jake can’t make it. “

She smiled and looked at her shoes. “So it’s just us, how fun. “

He smiled and said softly “Really, it’s great to see you, it has been awhile.”

A little louder he said, “Jake left us for a beer and frat party, totally last oligarchy. Miller miraculously passed his oral. You remember Miller?”

“Sure. He’s from Wisconsin right?”

“Ya, go figure chemistry and Miller.”

“Silly stuff, some guys go to those parties,” she said and smiled.

She was fumbling through her purse, then using her phone camera for a mirror, he adjusted her makeup just a bit, more for something to do to get past the moment.

She was half listening when she heard.

“Listen I thought we’d have a little fun, but I know you hate fun .. so I wanted to be upfront about it.”

Chuckling.. she said “What?”.

“Trust me, you’ll never regret our little evening of quiet reflection and cake.”

“Didn’t you mention fun?”

“..and fun. Loads of fun. Literally more fun than you can have in this state without a note from your parents.”

“Oh-kay, you’re on — let me see if I brought my heart pills, yep check, check and check. “

They were standing there on the plaza, less than a foot apart. Her hair blew and tickled his face.

She was playing along “Let’s go.” She said.

They began walking up Broadway.

For a moment they just walked, the sound of traffic in the city and the sound of heels on uneven concrete.

Ahead there was a rough patch where the rain, sun, and compression had crushed the center of a section of the sidewalk. Her steps grew a little irregular on the loose pavement and crumbled concrete.

In the store window to her right, he caught a look as she teetered on one foot then another when a stone turned her heel.

They made a slight turn and then took two more steps to the curb.

He said “lets grab a taxi?” and raised his right arm.

It was just about 6 and traffic was a river of yellow taxis up and down every street. Most of the duty lights were off.

Usually, the subway was how they got around. Taxis were slow, but a little less public but whenever it seemed like a taxi was just what you wanted it was impossible to find one.

Tonight they were lucky. Just ahead a cab door opened and a couple exited. In a quick few steps, they were inside.

As the door shut behind them it started to rain a little.

“Driver, please just takes us up Fifth Avenue, when we get close I’ll let you know.”

“I won’t go to Harlem.” The driver didn’t look like he would take an argument on this.

“No, we’ll get out before then.”

“So, what are we doing?” She asked with a smile.

“We’re going on an adventure, if you agree.”

The driver interrupted.

“There’s some big do on Fifth Avenue just so you know, traffic is lighter on second.”

“Fifth please, it’s a nicer ride.”

“You got it.”
